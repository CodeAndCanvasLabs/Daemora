# Food & Cooking Domain Reference

## 🔥 Trending Angles
- "I made [dish] every day for a week — here's what I learned"
- "Restaurant vs homemade: can I beat [famous dish]?"
- "The technique pro chefs use that home cooks ignore"
- "I tried every [food item] in [city] and ranked them"
- "Budget cooking: $30 for a week of meals"
- "The science behind why [dish] tastes so good"
- "Viral recipe test: does it actually work?"
- "One ingredient that makes everything taste better"

## ⚡ Hook Formulas
- "This takes 10 minutes and tastes like it took all day."
- "I've been caramelizing onions wrong for years. Here's the right way."
- "Every Italian grandmother does this. No recipe blogger ever explains why."
- "This $3 ingredient makes restaurant-quality [dish] at home."

## 🎥 B-Roll (Food is the most visual domain — be very specific)
- Overhead pour shot (sauce, oil, liquid)
- Sizzle shot (food hitting hot pan — must include sound note)
- Cross-section cut reveal (cheese pull, layer reveal)
- Hands-on prep close-up (chopping, kneading, plating)
- Steam rising from dish
- Final plating on clean background (hero shot)
- Time-lapse of cooking process

## Sound Design Note
Food videos LIVE on sound. Always note:
- "[SOUND: Loud sizzle as garlic hits oil]"
- "[SOUND: Crisp crunch of first bite]"
- "[SOUND: Bubbling of sauce]"
These are as important as the visuals.

## Tone Notes
- Warm, approachable, generous — like teaching a friend
- Acknowledge failures: "First attempt was terrible — here's what I changed"
- For ASMR food content: whisper narration, extreme close-ups, ambient kitchen sounds only
