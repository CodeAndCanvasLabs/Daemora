## Step 2: Analyze the Idea

Silently determine these before writing a single word:

### 2A — Detect Format
| Signal in Prompt | Format |
|---|---|
| "Short", "Reel", "TikTok", under 90s | Short-form |
| "YouTube", "video essay", no length given + complex topic | YouTube Long-form |
| "Corporate", "training", "onboarding", "explainer for clients" | Corporate/Training |
| "podcast", "interview", "conversation" | Podcast-style |

### 2B — Detect Style
Look for keywords, tone of request, and subject matter:

| Style | When to Use |
|---|---|
| **Cinematic / Documentary** | History, science, investigative, nature, "the story of X" |
| **Vlog / Talking Head** | Personal experience, opinion, day-in-life, reviews |
| **Faceless / AI Voiceover** | User says "no face", "AI voice", listicles, anonymous explainers |
| **News / Journalistic** | Breaking topics, current events, analysis, "what happened with X" |
| **Podcast / Interview** | Two-person conversations, expert breakdowns, Q&A formats |
| **Motivational / Hype** | Self-improvement, entrepreneurship, fitness, mindset content |
| **Satirical / Comedy** | Parody, roasts, absurd takes, "hot takes", entertainment |
| **ASMR / Calm & Slow** | Meditation, journaling, food content, peaceful tutorials |

If the user specifies a style explicitly, always honor it. If unclear, infer from the topic and domain.

### 2C — Detect Length

| Idea Type | Estimated Duration |
|---|---|
| Single tip, meme-adjacent, one concept | 30–60 seconds (Short) |
| Listicle, quick tutorial, explainer | 3–7 minutes |
| Deep dive, essay, review, case study | 8–15 minutes |
| Documentary, full course module | 15–25 minutes |
| Corporate intro / product demo | 2–4 minutes |
| Training module | 6–12 minutes |

## Style Execution Rules

### 🎬 Cinematic / Documentary
- Open with a wide establishing shot + ambient sound note
- Narration is measured, weighty — like a Netflix narrator
- Use act structure: Act 1 (Setup) → Act 2 (Conflict/Tension) → Act 3 (Resolution)
- B-roll is specific and visual: "Golden hour shot of an empty factory floor"
- Music swells at emotional peaks — note this in STYLE NOTEs
- Avoid "Hey guys" — open with a statement or a question to the universe

### 📹 Vlog / Talking Head
- Start mid-sentence or mid-action ("So I just got back from...")
- Casual, first-person, conversational — like talking to a friend
- Reaction moments matter: note "[laugh here]", "[pause, look at camera]"
- B-roll = what they're actually doing (packing, cooking, commuting)
- Authenticity over polish — imperfections are part of the style

### 🤖 Faceless / AI Voiceover
- No "I" statements — write in second person or third person
- Script must sound great read aloud by a neutral voice — no slang, no jokes that need delivery
- Every sentence needs a visual counterpart — nothing abstract
- Sentences short. Punchy. Clear. Under 15 words where possible.
- Listicle structure works best: "Number 5 will surprise you"
- B-roll must be ultra-specific: "Stock footage: aerial drone over Manhattan at night"

### 📰 News / Journalistic
- Open with the who/what/when/where in the first 10 seconds
- Anchor delivery: clear, neutral, authoritative — no filler words
- Use inverted pyramid: most important info first
- Expert quote slots: "[CUT TO: Expert soundbite — 10 seconds]"
- Caption: breaking news chyron style — "DEVELOPING: [headline]"
- End with: "We'll continue to follow this story..."

### 🎙️ Podcast / Interview
- Write both host and guest lines if interview format
- Host: short questions, good listening cues ("[nod] Right, and...")
- Guest: longer, story-driven answers
- Timestamp markers every 5–7 minutes for chapter editing
- Note: "[Clip this moment for Shorts]" on strong standalone quotes
- Outro includes "Where can people find you?" segment

### 💪 Motivational / Hype
- Open with a bold, confrontational statement: "You're playing small. And you know it."
- Short punchy sentences. Hammer the rhythm.
- Use "you" aggressively — every line speaks to the viewer directly
- Build to a crescendo — emotion should peak in the final third
- Music note: cinematic build, silence before the big moment
- End on a challenge, not a request: "So what are you waiting for?"

### 😂 Satirical / Comedy
- The premise IS the joke — state it deadpan, let it breathe
- Note comedic timing: "[pause 2 seconds]", "[cut immediately]", "[zoom in slowly]"
- Absurdist B-roll suggestions encouraged: "Stock footage: man typing furiously, cut to: cat on keyboard"
- Subvert expectations in every section — set up → subvert
- CTA can be self-aware/ironic: "Subscribe. Or don't. We're not your mom."
- Avoid explaining the joke — ever

### 🌿 ASMR / Calm & Slow
- Narration is soft, close-mic, unhurried — note "[soft voice]", "[whisper]"
- Long pauses are intentional: "[3 second pause]"
- Ambient sound is a character: "Sound of rain on window", "Gentle keyboard clicks"
- No hard cuts — crossfade everything
- Sensory language: describe textures, temperatures, sensations
- No urgency in the CTA: "Whenever you're ready, there's more below"

## Universal Script Quality Rules

- **Say it like you'd say it** — read every line aloud before finalizing. If it sounds stiff, rewrite it.
- **Specificity over generality** — "ChatGPT-4o" not "AI", "$4,200/month" not "a lot of money"
- **Earn every second** — if a section doesn't teach, entertain, or emotionally move the viewer, cut it
- **Name the viewer** — use "you" to create intimacy (except in News/Journalistic style)
- **Vary rhythm** — short sentence. Then a longer one that gives the brain room to absorb. Then short again.
- **Show, don't tell** — always pair abstract narration with concrete B-roll
- **One idea per section** — if you're explaining two things, split into two sections
